Nikola Tesla

The Man Who Electrified the World

Here’s a timeline of Nikola Tesla’s life:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (now Croatia)
  • 1875 - Enrolls at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, where he studies electrical engineering and becomes fascinated with electricity and physics.
  • 1881 - Moves to Budapest and works for the Central Telegraph Office. While walking in a park, he conceives of the rotating magnetic field, the foundational concept for his invention of the AC motor.
  • 1882 - Moves to Paris to work for the Continental Edison Company, repairing direct current (DC) power plants.
  • 1884 - Emigrates to the United States and begins working for Thomas Edison in New York. Despite their initial respect for each other, they soon clash over their differing electrical systems: Tesla favors alternating current (AC), while Edison is committed to direct current (DC).
  • 1887 - Secures funding and establishes the Tesla Electric Company. Files numerous patents for AC-based electrical systems, including the AC motor and transformer.
  • 1888 - Demonstrates the practicality of alternating current (AC) for long-distance electrical transmission, which catches the attention of industrialist George Westinghouse. Tesla sells his patents to Westinghouse, and the “War of Currents” against Edison’s DC system begins.
  • 1893 - Westinghouse and Tesla's AC system triumphs when it’s chosen to power the Chicago World’s Fair, showcasing AC’s potential to the world. Tesla also pioneers the development of wireless communication and remote control during this time.
  • 1895 - Builds the first hydroelectric power plant at Niagara Falls, which supplies power to Buffalo, New York, demonstrating the vast capabilities of AC electricity.
  • 1898 - Unveils a radio-controlled boat at Madison Square Garden, a precursor to modern wireless technology and robotics.
  • 1901 - Begins construction of the Wardenclyffe Tower, an ambitious project aimed at transmitting wireless energy across the globe. Despite its potential, the project is abandoned due to lack of funding.
  • 1909 - Tesla experiments with high-voltage, high-frequency electricity, leading to the invention of the Tesla coil, which is still used today in radio technology and other scientific applications.
  • 1931 - Celebrates his 75th birthday with widespread recognition for his work in electrical engineering, though many of his later ideas, like wireless power transmission, remain unfinished.
  • 1943 - Dies in New York City at the age of 86. The U.S. Supreme Court credits Tesla as the inventor of radio, recognizing his essential patents over Marconi.
